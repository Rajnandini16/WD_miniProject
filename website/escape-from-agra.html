The Legendary Escape from Agra

Following the Treaty of Purandar, Shivaji Maharaj, along with his young son Sambhaji, was compelled to visit the court of the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb in Agra in 1666. Aurangzeb, known for his cunning and arrogance, did not treat Shivaji with the respect befitting a ruler of his stature. In a calculated insult, he placed Shivaji in the ranks of lesser nobles.

Feeling deeply humiliated and betrayed by this treatment, Shivaji openly expressed his displeasure and stormed out of the court. Aurangzeb, enraged by this defiance, ordered Shivaji and Sambhaji to be placed under house arrest. They were confined to a guarded residence, effectively prisoners of the mighty Mughal emperor.

Confined within the walls of Agra, Shivaji knew that his life and the future of his nascent Maratha Swarajya were in grave danger. Escape seemed impossible, with Mughal soldiers constantly watching their every move. However, Shivaji, known for his sharp intellect and audacious planning, began to devise a daring plan.

He feigned illness, complaining of severe pain and weakness. To appease the guards and create a sense of normalcy, he requested permission to send out large baskets of sweets and fruits to Brahmins and religious figures in the city as a form of charity for his recovery. The guards, seeing no harm in this religious gesture, allowed it.

For several days, these large baskets went out, seemingly filled with treats. However, Shivaji and Sambhaji had secretly devised a way to use these baskets for their escape. Shivaji, a man of considerable build, squeezed himself into one of the larger baskets, while the young Sambhaji hid in another. Loyal Maratha soldiers, disguised as laborers, carried these baskets out of the guarded residence, mingling with the everyday traffic of Agra.

The Mughal guards, accustomed to seeing the baskets go out daily, remained unsuspecting. Once outside the city walls, Shivaji and Sambhaji emerged from their hiding places. They then embarked on a perilous journey, disguised as ordinary travelers, making their way through hostile territory back towards the Deccan.

The journey was fraught with danger. They had to evade Mughal patrols and navigate unfamiliar paths. Legend has it that Shivaji, despite his discomfort in the cramped basket, remained resolute and focused on his escape. His courage and the loyalty of his men were instrumental in their success.

News of Shivaji's audacious escape from the clutches of the Mughal emperor spread like wildfire across India. It was seen as a miraculous feat, a testament to Shivaji's cunning, bravery, and the divine protection he seemed to possess. This legendary escape not only secured Shivaji's freedom but also reignited the Maratha struggle against Mughal dominance and further cemented his image as an indomitable leader.

Even today, the story of Shivaji's escape from Agra is recounted with awe and admiration, a powerful example of his strategic brilliance and unwavering spirit in the face of seemingly insurmountable odds. It's a cornerstone of his legendary status, especially here in Maharashtra, reminding people of his courage and his commitment to Swarajya.
